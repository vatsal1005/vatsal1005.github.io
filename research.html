Research Interests

Matter Chern–Simons Theory

Remarkable for their demonstrated Bose–Fermi dualities, being a gauge theory, it was only treated within a particular choice of the gauge until our work [arXiv:2307.11020], which sets up the problem amenable to analysis over general genus-g spatial Riemannian manifolds. We look to compute the thermal free energy explicitly over the surface of a finite-volume sphere.

Finite Temperature Electronic Structure Theory

Only an operator-based many-body theory existed for fermionic systems. Within the path integral formalism, I developed a finite-temperature, non-equilibrium, non-perturbative, strongly-correlated, relativistic, many-body fermionic theory. The analytical solutions at the mean-field level admit a sensible zero-temperature limit. This is usually elusive to conventional treatment of systems without special symmetries preserved throughout, as Kohn and Luttinger show.

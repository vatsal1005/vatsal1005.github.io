Research Interests

Matter Chern–Simons Theory

My research focuses on large-N Chern–Simons theories coupled to matter fields, particularly exploring Bose–Fermi dualities in these systems. I am interested in developing computational techniques beyond the light-cone gauge, specifically using the temporal gauge formalism to study these theories in curved spacetimes and at finite temperature.

Dirac SYK Model

I investigate the Sachdev–Ye–Kitaev model with Dirac fermions, exploring its connections to quantum chaos, holography, and the physics of near-extremal black holes. This work aims to understand the emergence of conformal symmetry and thermalization in strongly correlated quantum systems.

Finite Temperature Electronic Structure Theory

I work on developing ab initio methods for computing electronic properties of matter at finite temperatures, with applications to warm dense matter, stellar interiors, and high energy density physics. This involves extending traditional ground-state density functional theory to thermal ensembles.

String Field Theory

I am interested in the formulation and applications of string field theory, particularly in understanding non-perturbative aspects of string theory and the structure of the string landscape. This includes studying vacuum solutions, tachyon condensation, and the role of gauge symmetries in string theory.
